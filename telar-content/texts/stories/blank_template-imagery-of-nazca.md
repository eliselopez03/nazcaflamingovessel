title: "Imagery of Nazca Culture"
---

As seen in Figure 2, present populations of Andean and Puna Flamingo are primarily concentrated in the highlands of Chile, Bolivia, and Argentina. But, there have been some spotted occasionally in Southern Peru. Though the flamingo and Nazca homelands do not coincide, and it is very unlikely for a ceramicist to have encountered this bird, it is not completely unrealistic that a Nazca traveler or guest interacted with a flamingo in their lifetime. 

 

There are many other examples of bird imagery in Nazca art. Birds on land were often associated with times of high moisture and the start of the agricultural season. They may have also signaled crop prosperity. Seabirds are also very common in Nazca artwork. They were known to have guided fishermen, who followed their flight patterns to navigate and locate fish below the surface. Many different species of birds were interpreted as manifestations of mountain gods. The flamingo, which lives in aquatic environments, but in the mountains instead of the sea, may have represented any combination of these meanings. As an animal that is exotic to the region, the flamingo may have been revered as a mythical creature or godlike being. The spiritual significance may have been the reason for this vessel to be adorned with flamingo imagery.
