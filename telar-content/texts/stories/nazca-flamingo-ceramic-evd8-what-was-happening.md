---
title: "What was happening in this time period that affected their ceramic craftsmanship?"
---

The time period heavily affected Nazca ceramic craftsmanship. Since farming and water management were essential as previously mentioned due to the desert environment, many ceramics designs reflect activities, such as planting fields, harvesting, and controlling water sources. Skilled Nazca artists created complex symbolic imagery by using geometric patterns, warm earthy colors, and bold surface design where members of their society could daily recognize. The frequent presence of finely decorated vessels in households suggest that these ceramics were not only used for ceremonial purposes but also played an important role in everyday life and consumption. These vessels were carefully made from clay, painted on terracotta surfaces, and fired in an oxidation atmosphere to produce vibrant colors.
