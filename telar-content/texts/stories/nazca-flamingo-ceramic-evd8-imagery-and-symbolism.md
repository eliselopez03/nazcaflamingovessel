****There are many other examples of bird imagery in Nazca art. Birds on land were often associated with times of high moisture, and the start of the agricultural season. They may have also signaled crop prosperity. Sea birds are also very common in Nazca artwork. They were known to have guided fishermen, who followed their flight patterns to navigate and locate fish below the surface. Many different species of birds were interpreted as manifestations of mountain gods. The flamingo, which lives in aquatic environments, but in the mountains instead of the sea, may have represented any combination of these meanings. As an animal that is exotic to the region, the flamingo may have been revered as a mythical creature, or godlike. The spiritual significance may have been the reason for this vessel to be adorned with flamingo imagery.****
