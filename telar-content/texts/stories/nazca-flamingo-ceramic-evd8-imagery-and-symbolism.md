---
title: "Imagery and symbolism of animals within the Nazca culture: "
---

**

To the Nazca people, animals carried significance beyond their physical form. Birds on land were often associated with times of high moisture, and the start of the agricultural season. They may have also signaled crop prosperity. Sea birds are also very common in Nazca artwork and were known to have guided fishermen, who followed their flight patterns to navigate and locate fish below the surface. Many different species of birds were interpreted as manifestations of mountain gods. The flamingo, which lives in aquatic environments, but in the mountains instead of the sea, may have represented any combination of these meanings. As an animal that is exotic to the region, the flamingo may have been revered as a mythical creature, or godlike. The spiritual significance may have been the reason for this vessel to be adorned with flamingo imagery. 

 

Within Nazca art lies many examples of Bird imagery. Perhaps the most famous archaeological site associated with this region is the Nazca Lines, a collection of geoglyphs that spans nearly 500 square kilometers. Several of these enormous geoglyphs have been identified to be depictions of various bird species. Another significant instance are Nazca feather garments. These intricate pieces were adorned with bird feathers, many of which were native to regions hundreds of kilometers away. This further proves the long-distance trade networks maintained by the Nazca, and the probability of a flamingo encounter.
