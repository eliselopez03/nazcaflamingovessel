---
title: "How did these ceramics survive?"
---

**Nazca ceramics have survived because they were made from durable fired clay and were preserved by the dry desert climate. The lack of moisture in the region helped protect many vessels from deterioration. Archaeologists have discovered these ceramics in burials, ceremonial sites, and domestic settings, showing that they were used throughout  their society rather than only elites. Nazca ceramics were part of a shared system that was accessible to all members of society, serving both  practical and ceremonial purposes. Their widespread distribution demonstrates how these ceramics were very important in expressing their culture. The preservation of these vessels provide both historians and archaeologists with valuable information about the Nazca agriculture practice, the Nazca beliefs, and Nazcas relationship with natural resources through ceramics.**![Shallow bowl with vencejo](https://eliselopez03.github.io/nazcaflamingovessel/iiif/objects/screenshot-20260602-at-115857-pm/screenshot-20260602-at-115857-pm.jpg)
