---
title: "Why were ceramics/pottery significant to the Nazca?"
---

Their culture relied heavily on religion and agricultural rituals which can be seen especially in the ceramics they created. Due to the lack of a written language, scholars believe that ceramic vessels played a significant role in communicating socially, religiously and ceremonially. Backed up by such scholars, "Visual and ethnographic analysis suggests the vessels served less as representations or illustrations, but rather constituted beings in and of themselves." (Tierney 1). These vessels were used not just as decoration but for ceremonies and burials, as well as a way for documentation on aspects of their society and nature.
